Earth


About

Planet Earth is the third planet from the sun in our solar system and the only known planet with life. It has a diameter of approximately 12,742 kilometers and a mass of 5.97 x 10^24 kilograms. Earth is a rocky planet with a thin atmosphere, and it has one natural satellite, the moon, which orbits around it.


Surface

The surface of Earth is composed of several layers, including the crust, mantle, and core. The outermost layer, the crust, is divided into several large plates that move slowly over time. The mantle is made up of hot, dense rock, and the core is divided into two parts, the outer core which is liquid, and the inner core which is solid.


Atmosphere

Earth's atmosphere is primarily composed of nitrogen (78%) and oxygen (21%), with trace amounts of other gases. The atmosphere helps to regulate the planet's temperature and protect it from harmful radiation from the sun. The biosphere, or the living part of the planet, is incredibly diverse and includes a wide variety of plant and animal life.


Rotation and Revolution

Earth has a rotation on its axis that takes about 24 hours, giving rise to day and night, and it takes about 365.24 days to complete one revolution around the sun, giving rise to the changing of the seasons. Overall, Earth is a unique and remarkable planet with a complex and interconnected system of geology, atmosphere, and life.
