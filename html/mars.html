Mars


About

Mars is the fourth planet from the sun in our solar system and is the second-smallest planet after Mercury. It has a diameter of approximately 4,220 kilometers and a mass of 6.39 x 10^23 kilograms. Mars is known for its reddish color, which is caused by rusty iron in its surface rocks. The planet is named after the Roman god of war, Mars.


Surface & Atmosphere

Mars is a rocky planet, with a solid surface that has been shaped by volcanoes, impacts, winds, crustal movement and chemical reactions. Mars has the largest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons, which is about three times taller than Mount Everest. Mars also has a huge canyon system, Valles Marineris, which is about four times longer than the Grand Canyon. Mars has a very thin atmosphere, composed mainly of carbon dioxide (96%), with trace amounts of other gases. Mars’ atmosphere is too thin to support liquid water on the surface, but there is evidence that water once flowed on Mars in the past. The temperature on Mars varies from -125°C to 20°C, depending on the location and season.


Moons

Mars has two small and irregular moons, Phobos and Deimos, which are thought to be captured asteroids. Phobos is the larger and closer moon, with a diameter of about 22 kilometers and an orbit of about 9 hours. Deimos is the smaller and farther moon, with a diameter of about 12 kilometers and an orbit of about 30 hours.


Rotation and Revolution

Mars rotates on its axis at a similar rate as Earth, completing one rotation in just over 24 hours. However, its revolution around the sun is much longer, taking approximately 687 Earth days to complete one orbit. As a result, the seasons on Mars are twice as long as those on Earth.
