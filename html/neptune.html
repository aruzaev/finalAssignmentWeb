Neptune


About

Neptune is the eighth planet from the sun in our solar system and is the farthest planet from the sun. It has a diameter of approximately 49,244 kilometers and a mass of 1.02 x 10^26 kilograms. Neptune is more than 17 times as massive as Earth, but has a similar density because it is also an ice giant planet. It is made mostly of gases and ices, with a small rocky core. The planet is named after the Roman god of the sea, Neptune.


Surface & Atmosphere

Neptune does not have a solid surface, but rather a dense atmosphere that gradually transitions into a liquid interior. The atmosphere of Neptune is composed mainly of hydrogen (80%), helium (19%), and methane (1%), with trace amounts of other gases. Methane gives Neptune its blue color by absorbing red light. Neptune has the coldest atmosphere of any planet in the solar system, with an average temperature of -200Â°C. Neptune also has the strongest winds of any planet in the solar system, reaching up to 2,100 km/h.


Moons

Neptune has 14 known moons, which are named after sea gods and nymphs in Greek mythology. The largest of these moons is Triton, which has a diameter of 2,710 kilometers. Triton is the only large moon in the solar system that orbits its planet in the opposite direction of its rotation. Triton also has a thin atmosphere and active geysers that spew nitrogen gas and dust.


Rotation and Revolution

Neptune rotates on its axis at a relatively fast rate, completing one rotation in about 16 hours and 6 minutes. However, its axis of rotation is tilted by 28 degrees from the plane of its orbit around the sun, which causes seasons on Neptune similar to those on Earth. Neptune orbits the sun once every 165 Earth years, at an average distance of about 4.5 billion kilometers or 30 astronomical units.
