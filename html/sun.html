Sun


About

The Sun is the star at the center of our solar system and is the largest object in our solar system. It has a diameter of approximately 1.39 million kilometers and a mass of 1.99 x 10^30 kilograms. The Sun is more than 330,000 times as massive as Earth, and contains 99.86% of the total mass of the solar system. It is made mostly of hydrogen and helium, with small amounts of other elements. The Sun is named after the Latin word for sun, sol.


Surface & Atmosphere

The Sun does not have a solid surface, but rather a layer of plasma that emits light and heat. This layer is called the photosphere and has a temperature of about 5,800Â°C. Above the photosphere is the chromosphere, a thin layer of hot gas that glows red during solar eclipses. Above the chromosphere is the corona, a huge halo of hot plasma that extends millions of kilometers into space. The corona has a temperature of more than one million degrees Celsius.


Moons

The Sun does not have any moons, but it does have eight planets and many smaller objects that orbit around it.


Rotation and Revolution

The Sun rotates on its axis at different speeds depending on its latitude. At its equator, the Sun completes one rotation in about 25 days. At its poles, the Sun rotates once every 36 days. The Sun orbits the center of our galaxy, the Milky Way, once every 250 million years. The Sun is about 26,000 light-years away from the galactic center.
